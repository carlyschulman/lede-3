Let's build a Big Mac

Franchises

Over 95% of McDonald's locations are franchises. Since McDonald'd does not directly dictate prices, franchisees have the authority to set their own prices.

Location

Location plays a big role in affecting the price of food, rent, labor and other factors based on local market conditions.

Rent

Franchisees have to pay rent which is expensive in expensive cities like New York. Who knew!

See if I had more time or planned better I would put in some stats about commercial rents in cities and how they vary across the country and how they've changed over the past few years.

Construction and Renovation

Sometimes you have to build things for restaurant.

Franchise Fee

McDonald's charges a $45,000 franchise fee to its franchisees when they are opening a new restaurant.

Launching a new McDonald's franchise costs around 1.3 to 2.3 million dollars according to Investopedia. AP style is not happening today.

Royalty Fee

McDonald's also charges a Royalty fee of 5% of gross sales.

This recently increased in 2024. Ah. Corporate greed.

Marketing and Promotions

Restaurants have to spend money to tell people to buy their food. They also have to participate in McDonald's little events.

I hope they do another Mariah Carey Christmas promotion.

Equipment and Maintenance

The restaurants need to buy machines to make their food. They also need to make sure these machines are working, except the ice cream machine.

They also hopefully clean the restaurant.

Supply Chain

I don't want to talk about this bc boring but food costs money and getting said food delivered also costs money.

Labor

When people work at the restaurants typically they need to be paid. They get paid more if there is a higher minimum wage or higher cozzie livs in the city.

Compliance

They need to spend money to make sure they are following all the rules. What rules, you may ask?

Market Position

They need to set their prices to compete with the other restaurants around them, which is often another McDonald's, sometimes only a few feet away.

So basically there is a lot that goes into deciding how much things cost. Sounds exhausting. But honestly I dont really care about all that, I just want to find the cheapest meal near me. That can be complicated in New York, where there are so many McDonald's locations and so much variation between them. Sometimes the pricing makes sense for the area, and sometimes it doesn't.
